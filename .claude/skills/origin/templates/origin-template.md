# Origin Template

Use this structure when generating origin.md from discovery interview.

---

```markdown
---
type: reference
status: active
created: YYYY-MM-DD
---

# Origin

## The Before (Ordinary World)

[Background - where they came from, professional history, what shaped them]

[What they were good at, what they learned, what drained them]

## The Pains (What Pushed Me)

[What was wrong with the old way]

[What frustrated them, what they couldn't stand anymore]

[The "push" — what they were running from]

## The Desires (What Pulled Me)

[What they wished existed]

[What they were running toward]

[The "pull" — freedom, legacy, impact, etc.]

## The Spark (Call to Adventure)

[The moment or gradual recognition that changed everything]

[What pissed them off enough to act, or what they finally realized]

[Core insight about themselves or their mission]

## The Leap (Crossing the Threshold)

[The moment of commitment]

[What they had to give up or risk]

[Why this is different from before]

## The Trials (Fears Faced)

[What almost stopped them]

[Fears, doubts, obstacles]

[How they reframed or overcame]

## The Wisdom (What I Bring)

[Bullet list of unique assets]

- [Years of experience] — [what it taught them]
- [Skill or perspective] — [why it matters]
- [Unique combination] — [what makes them different]

## The Values (What I Won't Compromise)

[Bullet list of non-negotiables]

- [Value 1]
- [Value 2]
- [Value 3]

## The Vision (What Success Looks Like)

[What winning looks like for them]

[Beyond money — freedom, impact, legacy]

[The endgame]
```

---

## Writing Guidelines

**Tone:**
- First person ("I", "my")
- Direct, not flowery
- Authentic voice — use their actual words when possible
- Short paragraphs, breathing room

**Length:**
- 500-1000 words total
- Each section: 2-5 sentences or bullet points
- The Wisdom and Values: bullet lists

**What to include:**
- Specific details (years, places, names)
- Direct quotes from the interview
- The "aha" moment or key insight
- Trench stories in The Trials section

**What to avoid:**
- Marketing language
- Vague platitudes
- Overly polished prose
- Third person perspective
