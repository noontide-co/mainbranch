# Question Bank

All interview questions organized by stage, with follow-ups and what they surface.

---

## Stage 1: Ordinary World

**Primary:**
- "What was your life like before this?"
- "Tell me about your professional background."

**Follow-ups:**
- "What did [X years in Y] teach you that most people don't know?"
- "What were you good at?"
- "What drained you?"
- "What was life like in the 'before' times?"

**What it surfaces:** Background, context, expertise, the "before" state

---

## Stage 1b: Pains & Desires

**Primary:**
- "What was wrong with the old way?"
- "What frustrated you most?"

**Follow-ups (Pains - the push):**
- "What were you running from?"
- "What couldn't you stand anymore?"
- "What was broken that no one was fixing?"

**Follow-ups (Desires - the pull):**
- "What did you wish existed?"
- "What were you running toward?"
- "What would have made everything better?"

**What it surfaces:** Motivation, the push/pull dynamic

---

## Stage 2: Call to Adventure

**Primary:**
- "What was the moment that changed everything?"
- "What was your epiphany?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What pissed you off enough to do something about it?"
- "When did you first think 'someone should fix this'?"
- "What was the last straw?"

**5 Whys technique:**
- Start: "I started this because..."
- Ask: "Why?" → get answer
- Ask: "Why is that important?" → go deeper
- Repeat 3-5 times until you hit bedrock value

**For gradual recognition:**
- "Not everyone has a dramatic moment. Was it more of a gradual recognition?"
- "When did you first notice something had to change?"
- "What was the accumulation of moments?"

**What it surfaces:** The spark, inciting incident, core values

---

## Stage 3: Refusal of the Call

**Primary:**
- "What almost stopped you?"
- "What held you back?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What were you afraid of?"
- "Why didn't you start sooner?"
- "What voice in your head said 'this won't work'?"
- "What did other people say?"

**What it surfaces:** Fears, doubts, obstacles overcome

---

## Stage 4: Crossing the Threshold

**Primary:**
- "What was the moment you actually committed?"
- "When did you cross the point of no return?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What did you have to give up or risk?"
- "Who or what gave you the push?"
- "What made you finally say 'I'm doing this'?"

**What it surfaces:** The decision point, the leap, commitment

---

## Stage 5: Road of Trials

**Primary:**
- "What was harder than you expected?"
- "What challenges did you face?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Who helped you along the way?"
- "What almost made you quit?"
- "What kept you going?"

**Trench story prompts:**
- "Tell me about a moment that reminded you why you do this."
- "Tell me about a customer interaction that stuck with you."
- "Describe a moment when you thought 'this is why I do this.'"
- "What's a small win that meant more than the numbers showed?"
- "What do customers say about you that makes you proudest?"

**Collect 2-3 trench stories.**

**What it surfaces:** Challenges, mentors, allies, growth, reconnection fuel

---

## Stage 6: Return with Elixir

**Primary:**
- "What do you know now that you wish you knew then?"
- "What wisdom do you carry from this journey?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What can you give others because of this journey?"
- "Why do you keep doing this?"

**Values questions:**
- "What would you never compromise on, even for money?"
- "What would you never do, even if it made money?"
- "What aspects of your work are non-negotiable?"

**Vision questions:**
- "If this works, what does your life look like in 3 years?"
- "What does success actually look like for you?"
- "If your business disappeared tomorrow, what would be lost?"

**What it surfaces:** Wisdom, values, vision, the "elixir" (the offer)

---

## Bonus Questions

**For deeper values discovery:**
- "What do you research when no one's watching?"
- "What intersections excite you when you find them?"
- "What decisions feel like discovery vs obligation?"

**For unique angle:**
- "What's the story you tell at dinner parties about why you do this?"
- "What do you see that others in your industry don't?"
- "If your brand was a person, who would it be?"

**For current state:**
- "What are you most excited about right now?"
- "What keeps you up at night?"
- "What's the next chapter of this journey?"

---

## Handling "I Don't Know"

When users get stuck:

| They say | You try |
|----------|---------|
| "I don't know" | "What pisses you off about your industry?" |
| "My story isn't interesting" | "What would your best customer say about why they chose you?" |
| "I haven't had that moment" | "Was it more gradual? A series of small moments?" |
| "I'm still figuring it out" | "What do you know for sure, even if everything else is uncertain?" |
