# Hero's Journey Framework

Joseph Campbell's "monomyth" from *The Hero with a Thousand Faces* (1949) describes a universal story pattern. Christopher Vogler simplified it to 12 stages. We use a 6-stage model optimized for founder origin stories.

---

## The Three Acts

| Act | Phase | What Happens | Origin Story Equivalent |
|-----|-------|--------------|------------------------|
| **I** | Departure | Hero leaves familiar world | "What were you doing before this?" |
| **II** | Initiation | Hero faces trials, transforms | "What challenges shaped you?" |
| **III** | Return | Hero brings wisdom back | "What do you know now that you wish you knew then?" |

---

## 6-Stage Model

### Stage 1: Ordinary World

**Campbell term:** The Ordinary World

**What it surfaces:** Background, context, the "before" state

**Primary question:** "What was your life like before this?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What was your professional background?"
- "What did [X years in Y] teach you that most people don't know?"
- "What were you good at? What drained you?"

**Output section:** The Before

---

### Stage 1b: Pains & Desires

**What it surfaces:** Motivation — the push and the pull

**Primary question:** "What was wrong with the old way?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What frustrated you most?" (pains - the push)
- "What did you wish existed?" (desires - the pull)

**Key insight:** Both matter. Pain is what you're running FROM. Desire is what you're running TOWARD. Together they explain full motivation.

**Output section:** The Pains, The Desires

---

### Stage 2: Call to Adventure

**Campbell term:** The Call to Adventure

**What it surfaces:** The inciting incident, frustration, spark

**Primary question:** "What was the moment that changed everything?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What pissed you off enough to do something about it?"
- "When did you first think 'someone should fix this'?"
- 5 Whys: Keep asking "why" until you hit a core value

**Handling gradual recognition:** Not everyone has a dramatic epiphany. Ask: "Was it more of a gradual recognition? When did you first notice something had to change?"

**Output section:** The Spark

---

### Stage 3: Refusal of the Call

**Campbell term:** Refusal of the Call

**What it surfaces:** Doubts, obstacles, resistance

**Primary question:** "What almost stopped you?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What were you afraid of?"
- "Why didn't you start sooner?"
- "What voice in your head said 'this won't work'?"

**Output section:** The Trials (fears faced)

---

### Stage 4: Crossing the Threshold

**Campbell term:** Crossing the Threshold

**What it surfaces:** The decision point, the leap

**Primary question:** "What was the moment you actually committed?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What did you have to give up or risk?"
- "Who or what gave you the push?"

**Output section:** The Leap

---

### Stage 5: Road of Trials

**Campbell term:** Road of Trials

**What it surfaces:** Challenges, mentors, allies, growth

**Primary question:** "What was harder than you expected?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Who helped you along the way?"
- "Tell me about a moment that reminded you why you do this." (trench story)
- "What almost made you quit?"

**Collect 2-3 trench stories here.** These are the fuel for soul.md.

**Output section:** The Trials

---

### Stage 6: Return with Elixir

**Campbell term:** Return with the Elixir

**What it surfaces:** Wisdom, values, the "why" behind the offer

**Primary question:** "What do you know now that you wish you knew then?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What can you give others because of this journey?"
- "What would you never compromise on, even for money?"
- "If this works, what does your life look like?"

**Key insight:** The Elixir = The Offer. What you sell is the distilled wisdom from your journey.

**Output section:** The Wisdom, The Values, The Vision

---

## Why This Works

1. **Universal pattern** — People intuitively recognize this structure
2. **Narrative framing** — "Tell me your story" feels natural, not like interrogation
3. **Surfaces values indirectly** — Values emerge through story, not abstract questions
4. **Captures transformation** — The journey from "before" to "after" is the whole point
5. **Built-in emotional arc** — Struggles and victories create connection

---

## Trench Stories

The testimonials and moments that keep founders going are scenes from their Road of Trials:
- Challenges overcome
- People helped
- Small victories that proved the journey was worth it

These are **reconnection fuel** — not for social proof, but for the founder to re-read when drifting into pure execution.
