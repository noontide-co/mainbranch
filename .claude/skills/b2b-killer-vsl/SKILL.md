# B2B Killer VSL Writer

Creates conversion-focused Video Sales Letter scripts using Jeremy Haynes' 7-step framework.

## CRITICAL RULE: Never Invent Facts

**Before writing ANY VSL, you must verify you have explicit confirmation for every claim.**

- Do NOT invent statistics, numbers, or credentials
- Do NOT assume pricing, years in business, or client counts
- Do NOT fabricate testimonials or results
- If information is missing, ASK for it before proceeding

**Example of what NOT to do:**
> If the context doesn't explicitly say "we carry $10M insurance" — do NOT write that in the script.

**What to do instead:**
> "I notice I don't have information about your insurance coverage. What amount do you carry? This could be a strong differentiator."

After gathering context, re-read every factual claim in the script and verify it against the source material.

---

## Context Required

Before writing, you need these details about the business:

| Required | What You Need |
|----------|---------------|
| **Business basics** | Company name, location, what they sell/do |
| **Target audience** | Who buys, their role/title, demographic (affluent vs general public) |
| **Pricing** | Actual price or price range (Haynes: state price upfront) |
| **Differentiators** | What makes them different — must be verified facts |
| **Proof points** | Years in business, client count, results — only if explicitly provided |
| **Founder/presenter background** | Why they're credible — only verified info |
| **Common objections** | What prospects typically worry about |
| **Who it's NOT for** | Disqualification criteria |
| **CTA** | What action should viewer take (form, call, etc.) |

**If any required context is missing, ask for it. Do not proceed with gaps.**

## The 7-Step Scripting Framework

Jeremy Haynes models the VSL after a natural sales conversation.

### Step 0: The 60-90 Second Summary (Tweet Version)

**[0:00 - 1:15]**

Summarize the ENTIRE pitch in the first 60-90 seconds. The viewer should be able to conclude if this is for them without watching the full video.

Include:
- What you do and for whom
- Key differentiators (1-2 max)
- Price range
- CTA

This is the "tweet version" of your VSL.

---

### Step 1: The Hook (The Opener)

**[1:15 - 1:45]**

Immediately articulate what you can specifically help with.

**Rules:**
- NO "Hey everybody, my name is..." — nobody cares about your name at the start
- NO "Stop scrolling" — this is not a social ad
- Jump directly into the value proposition
- For affluent audiences: short, direct, authoritative
- A poor opener causes steep drop-offs in the first seconds

**Good opener structure:**
> I'm [Name]. I run [Company].
> We [specific service] for [specific audience] in [location/niche].
> If you're [situation they're in] — I built this specifically to solve that.

---

### Step 2: The "Why" (Legitimacy)

**[1:45 - 3:00]**

Explain why you/your company are the right ones to do this.

**Include:**
- Background and expertise
- Genuine reasons for legitimacy
- How you came to do this work

**Avoid:**
- "Flex points" like press mentions that don't sell your ability
- Credentials that don't relate to solving their problem
- Anything that sounds like bragging without substance

---

### Step 3: Market Drivers (Buying Motives)

**[3:00 - 4:30]**

Use "Majority Hooks" to explain why people buy. Cascade from most common to minority reasons.

**Structure:**
> "The number one reason [audience] come to us: [majority hook]"
> [Elaborate on this pain point]
>
> "The second reason: [next hook]"
> [Elaborate]
>
> "The third reason: [minority hook]"
> [Elaborate]

Help the viewer identify their own situation within your presentation.

---

### Step 4: The Offer & The Price

**[4:30 - 5:45]**

Present your offer clearly. **State the price upfront.**

**Why state price (Haynes):**
- Hiding price to get more calls prevents pixel conditioning around qualified buyers
- Stating price forces unqualified leads to drop off before conversion events
- This ensures ad algorithm only receives data from people who can afford you

**Structure:**
> Here's what we do and what it costs.
> [Describe deliverables clearly]
> [State price range or specific pricing]
> [Explain any terms — contracts, setup fees, etc.]

---

### Step 5: Objection Handling

**[5:45 - 7:00]**

Proactively address common concerns.

**For affluent buyers:**
- Focus on hard-hitting, real-world questions
- Time and risk concerns
- Keep it direct

**For general public:**
- Longer, more empathetic
- Handle skepticism carefully

**Format:**
> "Let me address the questions I get most often."
>
> "[Objection as a question]"
> [Direct answer]
>
> "[Next objection]"
> [Direct answer]

---

### Step 6: Qualification

**[7:00 - 7:45]**

Explicitly state who this IS and ISN'T for.

**This is for you if:**
- [Criteria 1]
- [Criteria 2]
- [Criteria 3]

**This is NOT for you if:**
- [Disqualifier 1]
- [Disqualifier 2]
- [Disqualifier 3]

This encourages right-fit prospects and deters unqualified leads.

---

### Step 7: Call to Action

**[7:45 - 8:15]**

Clear instruction on what to do next.

**Structure:**
> If what I've described sounds like what you need, here's the next step.
> [Specific action — form, call, etc.]
> [What happens after they take action]
> [Restate: no pressure, just next step]

## Vocabulary Control

### For Affluent/B2B Audiences

**Forbidden words:**
- "Money" or "Cash" → Use "Capital" or "Liquidity"
- "Savings" → Use "Reserves"
- "Spend" → Use "Deploy"
- "Coupon" → Never use (use "Discount" if needed)
- "Guaranteed" → Viewed as low sophistication/scammy
- Income claims ("Make $10k in 90 days") → Never use

**Required tone:**
- Conservative, undershot claims
- Acknowledge viewer values time and efficiency
- Direct, no "yapping"

### For General Public

**Tone:**
- Highly empathetic but authoritative
- Acknowledge they may be "dipping into savings"
- Scarcity can work (if genuine)

## Designing for Demographic

| Feature | Affluent Buyers | General Public |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Length** | 2-15 minutes | 10-60 minutes |
| **Tone** | Direct, authoritative | Empathetic, handles skepticism |
| **Design** | Simple: white bg/black text | Professional, polished |
| **Claims** | Conservative, undershot | Can be bolder (but FTC compliant) |
| **Trust assets** | Real data, technical walkthroughs | Testimonials, screenshots |

---

## The "Spectrum of Results" (Trust Assets)

When including proof, don't just show best cases:

1. **Best Case** — The home runs
2. **Average Case** — What typically happens
3. **"Failed" Deals** — A deal that went wrong and how it was handled

This creates authentic trust and disarms skepticism.

---

## Production Format Notes

If providing visual/recording direction:

1. **Classic Talking Head** — High authority, eye contact, personal brands
2. **Google Doc Split** — Speaker on right, raw notes/doc on left, reading through live (feels authentic)
3. **Simple Slides (Affluent)** — White background, black text. No logos, no fancy borders. Simple > polished for rich audiences.

---

## Persona Constraints

The messenger is judged within 1.8 seconds.

**For High Finance/B2B:**
- Buttoned-up appearance (suits, neutral office)
- Flashy attire attracts unserious leads

**For Creative/Alternative:**
- Casual can work (black tee, etc.)
- But don't "over-design" for affluent — signals low trust

---

## Technical Notes (For Landing Page Context)

- **Hosting:** Wistia or Vidalytics (never YouTube/Vimeo)
- **Controls:** Always show play bar, allow 2x speed
- **Chapters:** Add chapter titles to video player (+37% engagement)
- **Thumbnail:** Key moment or value point, not "Stop Scrolling"
- **Metrics:** Play Rate (% who press play) and Engagement Rate (% consumed)

## Quality Checklist

Before delivering:

- [ ] Every factual claim is verified against provided context
- [ ] No invented statistics, credentials, or results
- [ ] Price is stated explicitly
- [ ] 60-90 second summary covers the full pitch
- [ ] Vocabulary matches demographic (no forbidden words for affluent)
- [ ] Clear qualification section (who it's for AND not for)
- [ ] CTA is specific and actionable
- [ ] Chapter markers are defined for video player

---

## Process

1. **Gather context** — Ask for any missing required information
2. **Verify facts** — Confirm every claim before writing
3. **Identify demographic** — Affluent or general public
4. **Write script** — Follow 7-step framework
5. **Review for invented facts** — Re-check every number, stat, credential
6. **Deliver** — Include chapter markers and production notes
