# Savior

An outside force arrives and the problem is gone. Tools, products, services that handle it for you.

## When to invoke

- B2B SaaS that automates a chore.
- Done-for-you services where the buyer's job is to *not* think about the work.
- Plug-and-play tools.

Do **not** invoke when the buyer needs to do significant work to use the offer (then you're closer to Wounded Healer or Broken Hero — you're handing them a tool, not a savior).

## Audience-current trap → reframe

The audience is in **Tragedy Mindset** ("this is just how it is, the chore eats my Tuesdays") or **Victim** ("the system is against me"). The reframe: "you don't have to deal with this anymore."

## Do-not-state list

- "We solve [pain] forever."
- "Set it and forget it." (Even if true, the cliche is a mark.)
- "Magical." "Effortless." (The image should carry effortlessness; never claim it.)
- "Game-changing." Banned at all times.

## Paired-imagery template

- **Hero:** a frustrated tableau of the chore (a desk covered in receipts, a calendar full of conflicts) + the same desk clean and empty, a single screen showing the product. Same camera angle, two states.
- **Mechanism:** a screenshot of the product doing the work + a human walking away from a desk, free to do something else.
- **Proof:** an outcome metric (specific: "saved 11 hours/week per founder, n=43") + a person spending those hours on something they care about.

## Headline formulas that work

- *"Stop [doing the chore]. We do it for you."*
- *"[Outcome the buyer cares about], without [the part they hate]."*
- *"The last [chore tool] you'll ever need."* (Use sparingly; ages fast.)

## Voice anchor

- Direct, declarative, no warm-up.
- Names the chore in the audience's words.
- Specifies what's automated and — honestly — what isn't.

## Anti-patterns

- Robot imagery.
- "AI-powered" without naming what the AI actually does.
- Hero illustrations of a person looking up at a glowing icon.
