# Headline Formulas

20+ formulas grouped by frame. The brief draft step picks 2-3 that match the dial and archetype. Test against the audience's words; if the headline doesn't sound like something they'd say or want to hear, it's wrong.

## Outcome frames

Lead with the change the buyer gets.

1. *"[Outcome] without [the part they hate]."* — David & Goliath, Savior
2. *"[Specific outcome] in [specific timeframe], [specific input]."* — Savior
3. *"From [current state] to [desired state]."* — Wounded Healer (sparingly)
4. *"Stop [doing X]. We do it for you."* — Savior
5. *"How [audience] [achieved outcome] without [common path]."* — Wounded Healer

## Problem frames

Lead with the pain the buyer feels.

6. *"Tired of [specific pain]?"* — Savior, David & Goliath
7. *"[Specific pain phrase the audience uses verbatim]."* — Wounded Healer
8. *"Why [common attempt] keeps failing."* — Broken Hero
9. *"The reason [audience] still [stuck state] (and how to fix it)."* — Wounded Healer

## Audience frames

Lead by naming who this is for.

10. *"For [audience descriptor] who've already tried [common attempts]."* — Broken Hero
11. *"Built for [specific audience], not [adjacent audience]."* — David & Goliath
12. *"For people who've outgrown [Goliath] but can't switch yet."* — David & Goliath
13. *"For the [audience] who [specific behavior]."* — Wounded Healer

## Differentiation frames

Lead with what makes this different.

14. *"The [category] that doesn't [Goliath's worst sin]."* — David & Goliath
15. *"We don't [convention]. Here's why."* — Dark Hero
16. *"[Goliath's product] is great. We do one thing they can't."* — David & Goliath
17. *"The [thing] [industry] doesn't want to admit."* — Dark Hero (use sparingly)
18. *"Run your [thing] in [unit] without [Goliath's friction]."* — David & Goliath

## Proof frames

Lead with evidence.

19. *"[Specific metric, n-anchored]. [Specific population]."* — Savior, Wounded Healer
20. *"[Customer name] used [offer] to [outcome]. Here's how."* — Wounded Healer
21. *"How we [outcome] [specific number] times in [period]."* — Savior

## Compound frames

Combine two of the above.

22. *"For [audience], [outcome] without [pain]."* — Audience + Outcome
23. *"[Pain]? [Specific differentiation]."* — Problem + Differentiation
24. *"We [proof], so you can [outcome]."* — Proof + Outcome

## Picking by dial

| Dial | Pick from |
|---|---|
| `convert` | Outcome, Problem, Proof. Compound frames preferred. |
| `story` | Audience, Wounded Healer / Broken Hero formulas. |
| `brand` | Differentiation, Dark Hero (sparingly), formulas that name the brand's position rather than promising a result. |

## Test gate

Before locking a headline:

1. Read it aloud. If you stumble, it's too long or jargon.
2. Imagine the audience reading it on a phone, half-distracted. Does it land in 2 seconds?
3. Does it use a word the audience uses (from `audience.md`)? If not, swap one in.
4. Does it make a promise the offer can't keep? If yes, soften.
